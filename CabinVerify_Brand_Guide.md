## Brand Foundation

**Tagline:** Local eyes. Neutral proof. Faster owner decisions.

**What CabinVerify is NOT:**

## Brand Research: What Trust Looks Like

Before defining CabinVerify's look, the following patterns were pulled from verification, inspection, and neutral third-party companies known for being trusted:

| Brand | Primary Color | What It Signals |
|---|---|---|
| BBB (Better Business Bureau) | Deep navy (RGB 0/90/120) | Authority, impartiality, institutional weight |
| CARFAX | Deep red + white | Bold facts, no-nonsense, documented truth |
| Underwriters Laboratories (UL) | Deep navy + white seal | Certification, standards, neutral authority |
| InterNACHI (home inspection) | Blue-green tones | Calm professionalism, not alarm-inducing |
| USAA / Allstate | Navy + gold | Trust, stability, long-term reliability |

**Common patterns across all of them:**
1. **Deep, muted primary colors** — never neon, never saturated. The brightness of a warning sign signals danger, not trust. Trusted brands lower the heart rate.
2. **One strong primary + one warm accent** — gold, amber, or cream add humanity without sacrificing authority.
3. **Clean wordmarks** — the company name carries most of the weight. Icons are simple and geometric.
4. **Seal, shield, or checkmark motifs** — signal verification, completion, neutral review.
5. **High contrast text** — dark on light or light on dark. No ambiguity.
6. **No repair tools, hard hats, or hammers** — those signal contractor, not neutral party.

**What to avoid:**
- Bright greens (looks eco/wellness, not professional verification)
- Orange or red (alarm colors — wrong psychological signal for a calming neutral service)
- Rustic/folksy fonts or textures (undermines authority, reads as small-town handyman)
- Clip art cabin illustrations (too casual for a trust-based document service)

## CabinVerify Color Palette

The palette merges **deep forest green** (Smoky Mountain landscape authority) with **slate stone** (neutral, documentation, permanence) and **antique amber** (warmth, the mountains' golden hour, credibility without coldness).

### Primary Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary Dark | Ridge Night | `#1C3829` | 28/56/41 | Logo background, table headers, footer |
| Primary Mid | Smoky Forest | `#2E5E3E` | 46/94/62 | Primary buttons, headings, key UI elements |
| Accent Warm | Appalachian Amber | `#9C7A3C` | 156/122/60 | Accent highlights, tagline, secondary labels |

### Supporting Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stone Gray | Ridgeline Slate | `#4A5568` | 74/85/104 | Subtext, borders, secondary table rows |
| Light Background | Morning Mist | `#F6F3EC` | 246/243/236 | Page backgrounds, alternating table rows |
| Near-Black Text | Deep Hollow | `#1F2937` | 31/41/55 | Body copy, all running text |
| White | Summit White | `#FFFFFF` | 255/255/255 | Text on dark backgrounds, clean areas |

### Color Don'ts
- Do not use bright or neon greens
- Do not introduce blue (that was the color this brand replaced)
- Do not add red/orange in primary placement — only as a severity indicator deep inside reports (e.g., "Urgent")
- Do not use more than 3 colors in any single layout element

### Color Rationale
Deep forest green was chosen over navy because:
1. It carries the same psychological weight (calm authority, lower heart rate) as navy blue
2. It is uniquely ownable in the STR verification space — no major competitor uses it
3. It visually grounds the brand in the Smoky Mountain landscape without being folksy
4. It reads as "serious and local" simultaneously — exactly the positioning CabinVerify needs

## Typography

### Font Roles

| Role | Font | Weight | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wordmark / Display | **Montserrat** | Bold (700) | Logo text, page headlines |
| Headings | **Montserrat** | SemiBold (600) | H1–H3 on website and reports |
| Body | **Inter** or **Source Sans Pro** | Regular (400) | All body copy, report text, website prose |
| Tagline / Callout | **Montserrat** | Light Italic (300i) | Tagline, pull quotes, scan summary labels |

Both fonts are free via Google Fonts and render cleanly on screen and in print/PDF.

### Type Rules
- **All caps for status labels only**: VERIFIED / NOT VERIFIED / INCONCLUSIVE / REFER TO VENDOR
- **Sentence case for everything else** — professional, not shouting
- Minimum body font size: 10pt in print, 16px on web
- Line height: 1.5–1.6 for body; 1.2 for headings
- Letter spacing: +0.05em on display headings for authority feel

## Logo Concept Direction

### What the Logo Must Do
1. Work at favicon size (16×16px) through billboard size without degrading
2. Communicate neutral documentation, not repair or inspection
3. Combine a "cabin" visual cue with a "verified/checked" signal — without being cluttered
4. Work in single color (for stamps, embossing, dark shirt printing)

### Recommended Logo Architecture
**Icon + Wordmark, horizontal layout as primary. Icon alone as secondary/favicon.**

### Icon Concept: The Ridge Check
A minimal mountain ridgeline (two or three peaks, clean geometric lines) with the right peak forming or containing a subtle checkmark or verification tick. The ridgeline is the "local" and "cabin/mountain" cue. The checkmark is the "verified/neutral proof" cue. Together they say exactly what the service does without words.

**Why this works:**
- The ridgeline is immediately Smoky Mountain / Appalachian
- Clean geometric peaks read at small sizes
- A checkmark embedded in the rightmost peak feels natural (a peak *is* a check shape)
- No hammers, magnifying glasses, clipboards, or houses needed
- No visual confusion with home inspection or contractor services

### Alternative concept (secondary):
A clean shield outline with a minimal cabin silhouette (simple A-frame or gabled roofline) cut into the center. Shield = verification/neutral authority. Cabin = STR context. Simpler to render but slightly more generic.

### Logo Color Versions Required
1. **Full color on light**: Ridge Night `#1C3829` icon + "CabinVerify" wordmark in Deep Hollow `#1F2937`, tagline in Appalachian Amber `#9C7A3C`
2. **Full color on dark**: Summit White icon + wordmark on Ridge Night background
3. **Single color dark**: All `#1C3829` — for stamps, embossing, single-color print
4. **Single color white**: All `#FFFFFF` — for dark shirts, dark headers
5. **Favicon**: Icon only, no wordmark, Smoky Forest `#2E5E3E` on white or transparent

## Voice & Tone Summary

| Attribute | Do This | Not This |
|---|---|---|
| Neutral | "Visible wear is moderate to severe" | "This is awful and needs replacing immediately" |
| Calm authority | "The reported issue was verified during the visit." | "Yep, it's broken!" |
| Plain English | "Owner can use this to make a faster repair decision." | "Pursuant to the inspection findings herein..." |
| Factual | "Split vinyl with foam visible at the front-left corner." | "Looks like it's been there forever." |
| Humble scope | "This report documents visible conditions only." | "Based on our comprehensive inspection..." |

**Three words that should describe every piece of CabinVerify communication:**
> **Calm. Neutral. Clear.**

## Brand Differentiator Summary (for website copy)

CabinVerify does not repair, quote repairs, or receive vendor kickbacks. That is a feature, not a limitation. Our value is that we have no financial stake in the outcome — only in accurate documentation. That is what makes the report trustworthy to owners, useful to property managers, and defensible for everyone involved.
